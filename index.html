Agreement Rate

Take a look at the pie chart that shows your rate of agreement. Closing deals is important; but if you agree 100% of the time, maybe you sometimes say "yes," when you really should walk away. Don't agree just for the sake of agreeing. Maybe you should be bolder by seeking agreements that may not be easy to reach, but would pay off handsomely if you do succeed.

Negotiation Type

Review the different kinds of negotiations that you engage in. What lessons should you take from one context and apply to another? What works in one case might not be appropriate in another. Are there situations where you need to be more agile so that you can adapt your strategy to the circumstance? (For example, value creation may be more or less important in a given case.)

Importance & Satisfaction

Research shows that negotiators with higher aspirations tend to get better results. That advantage comes with a cost, however; two costs, in fact. People with high expectations can feel disappointed even when they get a good deal. They also have a higher rate of impasse, if their aspirations are unrealistic. The takeaway: be ambitious while you are negotiating; but don't brood about the outcome when you're finished.
